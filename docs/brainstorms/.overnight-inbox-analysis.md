## 3. Patterns Noticed

### The "ADHD Capture-Then-Abandon" Pattern
**Observation:** ~60% of Inbox notes are single-sentence or paragraph captures that were never developed. They represent:
- Moments of insight ("knowledge debt", "pre-registering a project")
- Emotional states ("the futility of learning", "the cost of thought")
- Tool discoveries ("integrating LLMs", plugin lists)

**Pattern:** Capture happens in high-energy/curiosity moments, but ADHD prevents follow-through. Notes become "graveyards of good intentions."

**System Implication:** The current vault restructure with **Rule 3** (one clear idea, 2+ links, proper tags) is exactly the right medicine. It forces development, not just capture.

### The Research Notes Are Actually Good
**Observation:** Research notes (`interpretable machine learning.md`, `medical ML interpretability.md`, `taiwan-papers-gap-analysis.md`) are well-developed, structured, and actionable.

**Pattern:** When the topic is central to active work (PhD research), the notes get developed. The system works for work-related content.

**Implication:** The problem isn't the vault system - it's that personal/philosophical captures don't have the forcing function of "I need this for my thesis."

### Meta-Notes Age Badly
**Observation:** Every Obsidian setup note from 2025 is now obsolete. The vault has gone through 3+ structural iterations.

**Pattern:** Systems thinking notes become outdated as the system evolves. The Salvage Scan notes will face the same fate.

**Implication:** Meta-notes should live in `Meta/` with timestamps and explicit "supersedes X" links. Or accept that they're temporal and archive aggressively.

### Film Notes Don't Belong in Knowledge/
**Observation:** The film reflection notes (Kaze Tachinu, Porco Rosso) were in `02 - Knowledge/Literature/` but they're personal reflections, not developed literary analysis.

**Pattern:** Confusion about what "Knowledge" means. Knowledge = reusable concepts with clear scope. Film reflections = personal captures.

**Implication:** The vault restructure got this right - these belong in Archive or a separate `Personal/Media` folder.

### The "CE Template Empty Note" Pattern
**Observation:** Several notes are CE brainstorm templates (`cardiac output baseline results.md`, `smartcuff subproject ideation.md`, `arterial analysis ml pipeline code review.md`) that were created but never filled.

**Pattern:** Templates get created with good intentions during planning, then abandoned.

**Implication:** This is fine - they're placeholders for future work. But they clutter Inbox. Should live in project `sessions/` folders.

2. **Tool collecting without deploying** - Many plugin lists, framework explorations, but limited evidence of actual use
3. **Meta-work can become procrastination** - Several vault setup notes suggest time spent optimizing the system instead of using it

### Insights

1. **The vault restructure is targeting the right problem** - Rule 3 (one clear idea, 2+ links, tags) forces development, not just capture

4. **You're building a research vault, not a life vault** - The system works best for work. Personal captures need a different home or acceptance of ephemerality.
